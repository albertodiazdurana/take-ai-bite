# Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

We are committed to providing a welcoming and inspiring community for all contributors. We pledge to make participation in this project a harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of age, body size, disability, ethnicity, gender identity and expression, level of experience, nationality, personal appearance, race, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

## Our Standards

### Positive Behavior

Examples of behavior that contributes to creating a positive environment:

- Using welcoming and inclusive language
- Being respectful of differing viewpoints and experiences
- Gracefully accepting constructive criticism
- Focusing on what is best for the community and methodology
- Showing empathy towards other community members
- Providing helpful and constructive feedback
- Acknowledging contributions from others

### Unacceptable Behavior

Examples of unacceptable behavior:

- Harassment, intimidation, or discrimination in any form
- Trolling, insulting or derogatory comments, and personal attacks
- Public or private harassment
- Publishing others' private information without permission
- Spam, self-promotion, or off-topic content
- Other conduct which could reasonably be considered inappropriate in a professional setting

## Project-Specific Standards

Given the professional and educational nature of this methodology:

### Expected Conduct

- **Professional Communication:** Maintain professional tone in all interactions
- **Constructive Feedback:** Focus on improving the methodology, not criticizing individuals
- **Evidence-Based Suggestions:** Support proposed changes with reasoning or examples
- **Respect for Process:** Follow contribution guidelines and review processes
- **Quality Standards:** Adhere to established formatting and documentation standards

### Technical Discussions

- **Stay On Topic:** Keep discussions focused on methodology improvements
- **Cite Sources:** Reference documentation or research when making claims
- **Test Before Sharing:** Validate code and examples before submitting
- **Clear Communication:** Use precise language, avoiding ambiguity
- **Acknowledge Limitations:** Be honest about what approaches can and cannot do

## Responsibilities

### Project Maintainers

Project maintainers are responsible for:

- Clarifying standards of acceptable behavior
- Taking appropriate and fair corrective action in response to unacceptable behavior
- Removing, editing, or rejecting contributions that do not align with this Code of Conduct
- Communicating reasons for moderation decisions when appropriate

### Contributors

Contributors are responsible for:

- Following the Code of Conduct in all project spaces
- Reporting unacceptable behavior to project maintainers
- Respecting decisions made by project maintainers
- Helping maintain a positive and productive community

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies to:

- GitHub repository (issues, pull requests, discussions)
- Project documentation and examples
- Communication channels related to the project
- Public spaces when representing the project

## Enforcement

### Reporting

Instances of abusive, harassing, or otherwise unacceptable behavior may be reported by:

- Opening a private issue on GitHub
- Contacting project maintainers directly
- Using GitHub's reporting features

All complaints will be reviewed and investigated promptly and fairly. Reporters' confidentiality will be respected.

### Enforcement Guidelines

Project maintainers will follow these guidelines in determining consequences:

**1. Correction**
- **Action:** Private written warning
- **Trigger:** Minor unprofessional conduct or violation
- **Consequence:** Clarification of standards, request for apology

**2. Warning**
- **Action:** Warning with consequences for continued behavior
- **Trigger:** Violation through single incident or series of actions
- **Consequence:** No interaction with involved parties for specified period

**3. Temporary Ban**
- **Action:** Temporary ban from project interaction
- **Trigger:** Serious violation or sustained inappropriate behavior
- **Consequence:** No public or private interaction for specified period

**4. Permanent Ban**
- **Action:** Permanent ban from project community
- **Trigger:** Pattern of violations, serious harassment, or aggression
- **Consequence:** No further contribution or interaction allowed
